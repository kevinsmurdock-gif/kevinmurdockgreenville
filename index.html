Greenville Story

A Local Operator. A National Moment.

Kevin S. Murdock grew up in Greenville, South Carolina. Shannon Forest Christian School. The Citadel, Class of 1992 — Gold Star, walk-on soccer, early graduate. He's been building in the Upstate ever since. AstraZeneca's top South Carolina territory in the early 2000s. Then Premier Medical Laboratory Services, founded in Greenville in 2007.

In March 2020, Greenville residents read headlines about testing shortages across the country. What most didn't know: one of the first private labs in the United States cleared to run COVID-19 tests was already operating a few miles away on Pelham Road. Within weeks, Premier Medical offered free testing to every law enforcement officer, firefighter, and EMT/paramedic in the state of South Carolina. Then the N95 donations. Then millions reinvested into the community and workforce.

By 2022, the lab network had been praised at a televised US Senate press conference, toured by a second US Senator's senior staff, and announced a $51 million expansion through the SC Governor's office. Greenville didn't just watch the pandemic response. Greenville built it.

The Network

Greenville Was the Headquarters. The Network Was National.

The 6000 Pelham Road facility in Greenville, SC is often described as “the COVID lab.” It was more than that. It was the 50,000 sq ft headquarters of Diversified Medical Healthcare — a holding company operating six independently licensed clinical laboratories across five states and employing up to 450 people at peak.

On February 23, 2022, DMH announced a $51 million expansion to add 136,000 sq ft of new space at Garlington North in Greenville County, creating 185 new South Carolina jobs. The announcement was made at SCBIO 2022, covered by PRNewswire, and published on the official website of the South Carolina Governor. The SC Secretary of Commerce issued an on-the-record endorsement. Greenville County Council Chairman Willis Meadows was quoted: “The company's expertise...fits well here.”

This is what public economic development looks like: a PRNewswire release, a governor's endorsement, a county council quote, a commerce department statement. Premier Medical was the flagship at Pelham Road. But the impact was national — six labs, five states, customers in all 50 US states, and 6M+ test kits manufactured locally through CPT Medical. Greenville was the HQ. The network was national.
